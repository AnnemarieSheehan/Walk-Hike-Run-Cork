Coastal Walks

Charles Fort Coastal Path

Charles Fort Walk is a nice coastal path walk with fantastic views at every turn from Kinsale town to the Charles Fort. The walk begins at the fort, a trail winds down to the sea and continues along the bay as far as the Kinsale boat yard. Crossing the yard, the trail continues winding upwards with stunning views overlooking the bay. You then arrive at seclueded beach which offers the perfect resting place mid way on your hike or run. To continue the trail, follow the road a little way uphill until you see a small plank that works as a makeshift bridge across a small ditch. The trail continues uphill until you arrive at an open field, take a moment to breath the fresh air and stamp the gorgeous surroundings before turning around to head back to the fort

Mizen Head

Mizen Head is located at the extremity of the Mizen Peninsula in the district of Carbery in County Cork, and is without doubt one of the most stunning natural attractions this country has to offer. The visitor centre tells us that South, the Fastnet Rock Lighthouse, Ireland's Teardrop, was the last landfall seen by many emigrants to America and one of Marconi's first telegraph stations. Mizen Signal Station had the first Radio Beacon in Ireland, 1931. As well as breathtaking views, you can enjoy the exhileation of a walk down the cliffs offering up close vistas of the caves below. A visit here takes approx 1-2 hours and comes highly recomended.

Ballycotton Cliff Walk

The Cliff Walk is from Ballycotton village to Ballyandreen beach along a cliff track where you are flanked by meadows on one side and the ocean on the other. It starts at the end of Ballycotton village (at the y in the road take the right up the hill, the left takes you to the pier). The walk extends over 5 miles on a foot worn track along the cliff face with stunning views and passes Ballytrasna beach. There are many stiles to cross on the route (so not suitable for bikes or buggies)

Nature Reserves & Parks

Doneraile Wildlife Park

Doneraile park is situated 11km north east from the town of Mallow and is a treat for walkers, runners and families alike. There are 3 marked walking routes, the longest of which is 5 Kms long and follows the outer walls of the park. The Awbeg river runs through the park and there are a couple of bridges across the river. The park has mature trees, rolling hills and beautiful views of the house. In the center of the park you can find red deer are grazing. It is peaceful and quiet as no traffic comes through the park and is a must see for anyone visiting the nearby areas.

Glengarrif Nature Reserve

Glengarrif Nature Reserve is located XXX. A variety of trails are provided in the Glengarriff Woods Nature Reserve to suit a range of abilities. You can take a gentle amble along the River Walk (1km) or make the steep, but rewarding climb up to Lady Bantry’s Lookout (1km). Longer options include the Big Meadow circuit (3km), which takes you through woodlands and old grassland, or the more rugged Esknamucky Trail, with its wonderful views out over the trees to the mountains beyond. One of the breathtakingly beautiful spots in the park is The waterfall, which is especially impressive after rain, is on the Canrooska River which flows down to meet the main Glengarriff River (.05km).

Gougane Barra National Park

This is Ireland’s first National Park, and is a favourite spot to unwind and get away from it all in the most beautiful and calm settings – what a most majestic part of the world, bordered by 3 mountain ranges and the extensive River Lee Valley!. The forest park comprises some 350 acres and views are breath-taking at every turn and at all hours of the day. It is a much-favoured location for walkers and hikers; there are challenging walks up the slopes, but rewarded by the amazing views of mountain lake and forests, For the serious hill walkers there are the steep hills surrounding the park.

Cork City Walks

Blackrock - Mahon loop

This is one of the most popular locations in Cork City for running and walking. Located just 3 kms east of the city centre, most of the route is on public walkways and is mostly free of traffic. It has largely flat with nice scenic views out over the river and harbour areas. Very easy to navigate as you are either following the river or staying on the public walkways. The route is shared with cyclists, walkers, runners and dog walkers but is wide and easy to traverse despite multiple activies taking place along the route. This is a perfect way to get out in the city while still enjoying fresh air away from city traffic.

University Walk

The University Walk traces the development of Cork from its medieval marshy roots, to the expansion of the City as a prosperous hub of industry and commerce. It emphasises the special bonds between ‘town and gown’, exploring associated institutional buildings and the centre of learning in the region, University College Cork (UCC). In addition this walk engages with the “Green” element of the City. The University Walk weaves it way through Bishop Lucey Park, the formal lawns of the UCC campus, and the gardens of Fitzgerald’s Park before strolling through the leafy shade of the Mardyke. It also meanders along Slí Cumman na mBan, which showcases the wildlife and natural splendour of the River Lee and the breadth of biodiversity still present in the heart of City.

Oldcourt Garryduff

Oldcourt or sometimes referred to locally as Garryduff, is a mixed woodland of approximately 26 hectares, located close to the southern suburbs of Cork city, namely Rochestown and Douglas. It has historical links back to eminent families from Cork such as the Ronaynes, Goolds and O’ Sheas. Notable features include walls and some old ruins associated with the adjoining estate and a number of small streams that flow through the woodland on their way out to Cork Harbour. There are significant sections of old broadleaved woodland to be found throughout the property along with stands of conifer trees at the southern end. There are some wonderful mature specimen Horse Chestnuts and Sycamore along the verges of the forest tracks that traverse the woods. A surprising find so close to the city. Enjoy!
